Overview

Image-to-video (I2V) models often generate videos that remain overly static, compared to text-to-video (T2V) models. While prior approaches mitigate this issue by weakening or modifying the image-conditioning signal, they often require additional training or sacrifice fidelity to the reference image.

We identify reference-frame dominance in self-attention — the tendency of non-reference frames to over-attend to the reference frame — as the mechanism behind motion suppression. We propose DyMoS (Dynamic Motion Slider), a training-free and model-agnostic method that rebalances the attention pathway from generated frames to the reference frame during the initial denoising steps. DyMoS preserves image fidelity while enabling continuous control over motion strength through a single scalar parameter.

✓ Training-free — no model parameters touched
✓ Model-agnostic — applies to off-the-shelf I2V backbones
✓ Continuous control — a single scalar slides motion strength
